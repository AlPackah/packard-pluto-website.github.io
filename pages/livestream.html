Join Us for an exclusive Pluto Livestream with Neil DeGrasse Tyson

Come to the Smithsonian Air and Space Museum IMAX theater to get an exclusive peak at the planet Pluto from NASA’s very own New Horizons satellite. New Horizons has been trekking towards Pluto since 2007, and will be in optimal position this July to send video feedback to earth.

Neil DeGrasse Tyson will be narrating the livestream as well as answering a few questions from both the audience and twitter! To tweet your question, use the hashtag #PlutoLivestream.

  • NASA’s New Horizons

    NASA's New Horizons spacecraft has launched from Cape Canaveral Air Force Station in Florida aboard a fast-moving Atlas V rocket.

    The spacecraft has been hurdling toward Pluto for almost a decade now. It took three tries to get the spacecraft on its journey.

  • The Voyage

    The first 13 months of the journey were spent in preparation for New Horizon's encounter with Juipter down the road.

    On Februray 28th, 2007 New Horizons passed the Red Giant within 32 Jupiter radii of the planet at 51,000MPH.

  • New Horizons Set to Wake Up for Pluto Encounter

    “New Horizons is healthy and cruising quietly through deep space – nearly three billion miles from home – but its rest is nearly over,” says Alice Bowman, New Horizons mission operations manager. “It’s time for New Horizons to wake up, get to work, and start making history.”

Guest Speaker

Neil DeGrasse Tyson

Tyson's professional research interests are broad, but include star formation, exploding stars, dwarf galaxies, and the structure of our Milky Way. Tyson is the recipient of eighteen honorary doctorates and the NASA Distinguished Public Service Medal, the highest award given by NASA to a non-government citizen.

His contributions to the public appreciation of the cosmos have been recognized by the International Astronomical Union in their official naming of asteroid 13123 Tyson. Mr. Tyson has also recently confirmed that his favorite Disney movie is indeed Wall-E.
